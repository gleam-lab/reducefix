### Explanation of Fixes:
1. **Index Conversion**:
   - The `lower_bound` and `upper_bound` functions return iterators, which are 0-based indices. The code was incorrectly assuming they were 1-based indices. I converted the indices to 0-based by subtracting `a.begin()` and then adjusted back to 1-based when accessing `c` (since `c` is constructed based on 1-based indices of `b`).

2. **Edge Case Handling**:
   - When `r_i` equals `n + 1` (which means `r` is greater than all elements in `a`), we correctly subtract from `c[n]` (which holds the cumulative sum of all `b` elements).
   - Similarly, when `l_i` equals 1 (which means `l` is less than or equal to all elements in `a`), we correctly start the sum from `c[0]` (which is 0 due to the initialization of `c`).

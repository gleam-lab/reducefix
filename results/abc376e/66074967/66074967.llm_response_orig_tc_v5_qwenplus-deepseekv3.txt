### Approaches

2. **Prefix Sums**: After sorting, we compute prefix sums of the \(B_i\) values to quickly calculate the sum of any \(K\) elements.
3. **Priority Queue for Top K Elements**: For each element \(A_i\) (from \(K\) to \(N\)), we consider it as the max \(A\) in the subset. We then need the sum of the top \(K-1\) \(B_i\) values from the previous \(i-1\) elements, plus the current \(B_i\). This can be efficiently managed using a max-heap (priority queue) to keep track of the largest \(B_i\) values.


The problem involves placing a new piece on a grid such that it is not captured by any of the existing pieces. The grid is \(N \times N\) in size, and there are \(M\) existing pieces on it. The challenge is to determine how many empty squares are left where the new piece can be placed safely.

### Key Observations:
1. **Capture Mechanism**: A piece placed at (i, j) can capture pieces on the same row, column, and two types of diagonals (i+j and i-j).
2. **Initial Calculation**: At first, the number of safe squares can be calculated by subtracting the number of occupied rows, columns, and squares from the total squares \(N^2\).
3. **Adjustments for Captures**:
   - For each pair of existing pieces (a, b) and (c, d):
     - If they are on the same row, column, or diagonal, further safe squares are reduced.
     - This is due to the overlap of their capture ranges.

### Solution Strategy:
1. **Counting Initial Unsafe Squares**:
   - Count the number of occupied rows, columns, and squares directly.
2. **Handling Capture Overlaps**:
   - For each pair of existing pieces, check if they can capture each other.
   - Subtract the overlapping safe squares accordingly.
3. **Output the Result**:
   - The final answer will be the total number of squares minus the number of unsafe squares.

### Implementation Steps:
1. Parse the input to get the grid size \(N\), number of pieces \(M\), and their positions.
2. Create a function to calculate the number of overlapping squares between two rows or columns.
3. Iterate over all pairs of pieces and adjust the count of safe squares based on their captures.
4. Print the final count of safe squares.

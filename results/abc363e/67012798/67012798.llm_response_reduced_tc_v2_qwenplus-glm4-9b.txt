
### Problem Analysis

The problem involves simulating the process of a rising sea level on an island represented as a grid. Each cell in the grid has an elevation, and cells adjacent (vertically or horizontally) to the sea or to another cell that has already sunk will also sink if their elevation is not greater than the sea level. The task is to compute the area of the island that remains above the sea level for each year over a given number of years.

### Solution Strategy

The solution involves simulating the sinking of the island over the given years. The key steps are:

1. **Initialization**: Read the grid dimensions, sea level rise, and the initial elevation grid.
2. **Sea Edge Identification**: Identify the cells that are already at sea level or are on the boundary (which will be automatically considered as sea level).
3. **Simulation**: For each year, simulate the sinking of cells based on the current sea level. Use a queue to process cells that sink at each level of the sea and propagate the sinking effect to adjacent cells.
4. **Output**: After simulating for each year, output the area of the island that remains above the sea level.

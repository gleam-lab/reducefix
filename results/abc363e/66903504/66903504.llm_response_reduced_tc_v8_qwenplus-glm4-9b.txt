
### Problem Analysis
The problem involves simulating the process of sea level rise on an island represented by a grid. Each cell in the grid has an elevation, and depending on its elevation and its adjacency to the sea or submerged cells, it may itself become submerged in subsequent years.

### Solution Strategy
The solution involves the following steps:
1. **Initialization**: Read the grid dimensions, the number of years for which the simulation is required, and the initial elevations of all grid cells.
2. **Edge Cells Handling**: Since the cells at the edge of the grid are always submerged (either initially or due to their low elevation), they are marked as such.
3. **Breadth-First Search (BFS)**: For each year, a BFS is initiated from the edge cells that were submerged in previous years. During the BFS, cells are marked as submerged if their elevation is less than or equal to the current sea level plus one. This marking is updated in a priority queue to ensure that cells are processed in order of their elevation.
4. **Remaining Area Calculation**: After each BFS, the area of the grid cells that remain submerged is calculated and subtracted from the total area to get the area of the island that remains above the sea level.
5. **Output**: The remaining area for each year is printed.

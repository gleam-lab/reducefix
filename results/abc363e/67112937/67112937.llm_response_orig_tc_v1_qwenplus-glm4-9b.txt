
The problem involves simulating the flooding of an island as the sea level rises over a period of years. The island is represented as a grid with given elevations, and sections that become submerged due to the rising sea level will sink. The goal is to determine the area of the island that remains above sea level after each year of rising sea level.

2. **Yearly Simulation:**
   - For each year up to Y:

     - For each section that becomes submerged due to the new sea level, mark it and all its adjacent sections (up, down, left, right) that have an elevation not greater than the sea level as submerged in the next step.
     - Keep track of the sections that will sink in the next step to start the BFS with them in the subsequent year.

3. **Calculating Remaining Area:**
   - After each year's simulation, the area that remains above sea level is the total area minus the number of submerged sections.

4. **Output:**
   - Print the remaining area for each year.

    int sea_level = 0;

        int submerged_area = 0;
        queue<pair<int, int>> current_sinks;

        for (int i = 0; i < H; ++i) {

                            submerged[ni][nj] = true;
                            ++submerged_area;
                        }

            }

        remaining_area -= submerged_area;

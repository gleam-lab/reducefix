2. **Check Feasibility**: The core idea is to ensure that for the \( N \) toys, we can match \( N-1 \) toys to the \( N-1 \) existing boxes such that each of these toys is placed in a box of size not smaller than the toy. The remaining toy will be placed in the new box of size \( x \). The minimal \( x \) is the size of the largest remaining toy that cannot be placed in any of the existing boxes, which corresponds to the largest toy in \( A \) that is not matched to any box in \( B \).

3. **Matching Toys to Boxes**: We need to pair the largest \( N-1 \) toys with the largest \( N-1 \) boxes.

The initial approach in the code attempts to sort the array and then greedily remove either the leftmost or rightmost elements based on the local differences between adjacent elements. However, this approach is flawed because it doesn't consider the global impact of removing elements on the overall range (max - min) of the remaining sequence.

To fix this, we need to consider all possible ways to remove K elements such that the remaining sequence's range is minimized. The optimal way is to either remove the K smallest elements, remove the K largest elements, or a combination of removing some from the left and some from the right. 

Here's the corrected approach:
1. Sort the array.
2. Consider all possible combinations of removing `l` elements from the left and `K - l` elements from the right, where `l` ranges from 0 to K.
3. For each combination, compute the range as `A[N - 1 - (K - l)] - A[l]` and keep track of the minimum range found.

This approach ensures that we explore all possible ways to remove K elements and find the one that minimizes the range of the remaining elements.

4. **Output**: The minimum range found is printed as the result.

### Approaches
1. **Understanding the Attack Pattern**: The attack pattern depends on the value of T. For each enemy, the attack alternates between decreasing health by 1 (when T is not a multiple of 3) and by 3 (when T is a multiple of 3). This means that every 3 attacks, the total damage dealt is 1 + 1 + 3 = 5.
2. **Efficient Calculation**: For each enemy, we can calculate the number of full cycles (3 attacks) and the remaining attacks needed to reduce its health to zero. Each full cycle reduces the health by 5, and the remaining attacks can be either 1 or 2 more attacks if the remaining health is not zero after the full cycles.
3. **Tracking T for Each Enemy**: For the i-th enemy, the time T must account for all previous enemies' attacks. The key insight is that the i-th enemy's attacks start after all previous enemies have been defeated.

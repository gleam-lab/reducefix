After analyzing the crash and code, I can identify the root cause:

The crash is a heap-use-after-free in `timelib_error_container_dtor` called from `php_date_initialize`. The issue is in the `update_errors_warnings` function where there's a double-free situation.

The bug occurs because:
1. The function first checks and frees `DATEG(last_errors)` if it exists
2. Later, after potentially assigning `*last_errors` to `DATEG(last_errors)`, it then calls `timelib_error_container_dtor(*last_errors)` 
3. This creates a use-after-free if `*last_errors` was assigned to `DATEG(last_errors)` in the previous step

The fix is to avoid calling `timelib_error_container_dtor(*last_errors)` when we've just stored that pointer in `DATEG(last_errors)`, as it will be freed later.

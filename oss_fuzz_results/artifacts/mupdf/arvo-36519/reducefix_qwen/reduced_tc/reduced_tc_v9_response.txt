The problem occurs when accessing `pixmap->w` and `pixmap->h` after potentially freeing the pixmap in the scaling section. The fix needs to ensure we don't access pixmap members after it might have been dropped.

The root cause is in the image scaling logic where we drop the original pixmap and replace it with scaled, but then later try to access its dimensions. We need to capture the dimensions before potentially dropping the pixmap.
